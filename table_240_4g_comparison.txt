COMPARISON: CEC Table 240.4(G) vs NEC Table 240.4(G)
====================================================

DIFFERENCES FOUND:

1. **Air-conditioning and refrigeration equipment circuit conductors**
   - NEC 2023: Article "440, Parts III, IV, VI"
   - CEC 2022: Article "440, Parts III, VI"
   - DIFFERENCE: CEC REMOVES Part IV reference

2. **Control and instrumentation circuit conductors (Type ITC)**
   - NEC 2023: Article "335", Section "335.9"
   - CEC 2022: Article "727", Section "727.9"
   - DIFFERENCE: CEC changes article from 335 to 727

3. **Remote-control, signaling, and power-limited circuit conductors**
   - NEC 2023: Article "725", Section "724.43, 724.45, 725.60, and Chapter 9, Tables 11(A) and 11(B)"
   - CEC 2022: Article "725", Section "725.43, 725.45, 725.121, and Chapter 9, Tables 11(A) and 11(B)"
   - DIFFERENCE: CEC uses "725.43, 725.45, 725.121" vs NEC's "724.43, 724.45, 725.60"
   - NOTE: NEC appears to have typo "724.43" instead of "725.43"

METADATA DIFFERENCES:

CEC 2022:
- Has "california_amendment": true
- Has "amendment_type": "delta"
- Description explicitly mentions "California amendment (delta) indicates modifications from the base NEC"

NEC 2023:
- No california_amendment field
- Description is generic NEC description

SUMMARY:
========
Table 240.4(G) exists in BOTH codes but California has modified:
1. Article reference for HVAC (removed Part IV)
2. Article reference for ITC conductors (335 â†’ 727)
3. Section references for remote-control/signaling circuits (different sections)

These are the "unique to California" aspects that the agent should identify.
